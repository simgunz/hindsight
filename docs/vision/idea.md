# Idea

Free-form brain dump of the original idea. No structure required — this is the
raw "why", preserved as captured.

## The problem

I work out in the gym and want to film myself to review my form. I prop my phone
on a gorilla tripod and record while I exercise. Reviewing a normal video is
cumbersome: I have to stop the recording, walk to the phone, open the gallery,
watch it, then delete it. Too many steps — and I have to physically handle the
phone every single time, which breaks my flow.

## What I actually want

A **delayed mirror**. The camera films continuously and the screen always shows
me from a fixed number of seconds ago. I do my set, glance at the propped phone,
and watch my last rep play back on a delay — hands-free, without touching
anything. Nothing is ever recorded or saved. This hands-free delayed loop is the
single most important feature.

## Who it's for

Me, and people like me: solo athletes who train alone and want to check their own
form with no partner to film them. Gym lifters, archers, and similar.

## Why a delay

By the time I finish an effort and look at the screen, the delayed playback should
be showing what I just did. A gym set lasts 60–90s, so I'd set the delay to ~60s.
An archery cycle (position, shoot, watch) is shorter, ~30s. So the delay must be
tunable, ideally with presets per activity.

## Other things I want

- Scrub back to rewatch a moment, possibly several times in a row.
- Double-tap to jump back to the base delay.
- Single-tap to pause and hold a frame.
- A live front-camera mode (mirrored, zero delay) to watch myself in real time.

## Rough scope

Most important: the hands-free delayed loop. Secondary: tunable delay + presets,
scrub gestures, the live mirror. Throughout, the UI should be clean, minimal,
distraction-free, and gesture-driven — readable from a few meters away in a gym.
